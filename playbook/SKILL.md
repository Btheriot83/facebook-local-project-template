# Facebook Local Business

## Overview

This playbook is purpose-built for local service operators where revenue is driven by calls, bookings, and filled calendars, not online checkout volume.
Each section maps directly to a local booking workflow so your content system produces appointments, not just reach.
You keep one operating model and adapt it with `{{PLACEHOLDER}}` fields instead of rebuilding strategy for every niche.
That lets you deploy the same framework across diesel, contractors, medspas, HVAC, plumbing, clinics, and roofing with minimal rewrite.
Run this correctly and you can build a Facebook system that supports 10-20 qualified service inquiries per month from organic activity.
The operating target is booked work: call volume, message-to-book conversion, and faster follow-up on high-intent leads.
You should implement this in 2026 because generic business posting no longer gets consistent organic distribution.
AI slop is saturating feeds, so authentic local proof from real jobs now carries more relative weight.
Meta's ranking is rewarding local-intent signals like on-site footage, before/after context, and real customer outcomes.
Most local competitors still rely on stock graphics and generic promos, which leaves room for operators who publish real field evidence.

## Research Basis (fresh)

Use these principles as the default baseline unless platform behavior materially changes.

### Primary references
- **Meta for Business — Dec 2025**: Reels hooks matter in the first seconds; test text, visuals, and audio together.
- **Meta for Business — Dec 2025**: native Reels creative outperforms repurposed generic creative; use vertical-first, sound-aware storytelling.
- **Meta UTIS recommendation model — Jan 2026**: Facebook now surveys users in-feed on how well content matches their interests. Reported lifts in quality ratings and engagement mean **high-quality, interest-fit Reels** matter more and clickbait gets punished faster.
- **Meta video measurement change — Jan 26, 2026**: 10-second video metrics were deprecated. Track **views** and **viewers**, not legacy 10-second benchmarks.
- **Meta originality + AI disclosure enforcement — Mar 2026**: unoriginal reposts, watermarked content, low-value reactions, cosmetic edits, and realistic AI media without disclosure can lose reach, recommendation eligibility, or monetization.
- **Meta traffic shift — Mar 2026**: Facebook referral traffic to external sites increased sharply, so outbound traffic can work again when used strategically.
- **Link-post limits — Dec 2025 policy, effective in 2026**: non-Meta Verified profiles should assume a practical cap of roughly **2 organic link posts per month**; Verified accounts have more freedom.
- **Business.com Facebook 2026 — Jan 2026**: local businesses should use community posts, Page optimization, video, and customer-service responsiveness.

### Field notes / directional signals
- **Reddit / operator reports (2025–2026)** repeatedly point to:
  - Reels still carrying more organic reach than standard posts
  - Groups outperforming Page-only posting for discussion/reach
  - early real comments helping momentum
  - saves, shares, and substantive comments carrying more weight than likes
  - same-day uploads outperforming older recycled content
- **Feed composition trend**: up to roughly half of Feed can be non-followed recommendations, so every post has to earn attention beyond your existing audience.
- **Public X snippets were low-signal in this session**. Directionally, they reinforced that retention/watch time and creative variation matter, but do **not** overweight X chatter over Meta’s own guidance.

## Core Facebook Model

Think in four lanes:

1. **Reels = discovery**
   - bring in non-followers
   - win attention fast
   *Revenue role:* Reels earn first impressions from cold local audiences; measure them on comment and message volume per Reel, not likes.

2. **Feed posts = trust**
   - explain
   - prove
   - localize
   *Revenue role:* Feed reduces booking friction by answering buyer objections with proof, so more profile visits convert into calls and messages.

3. **Stories = retention**
   - stay top-of-mind
   - show activity
   - create lightweight replies
   *Revenue role:* Stories keep warm prospects active between posts and create low-friction reply paths that move to message threads and bookings.

4. **Links / CTA posts = action**
   - drive calls, messages, bookings, quote requests
   - use sparingly
   *Revenue role:* Action posts convert built trust into contactable leads and booked work, so judge them on calls, messages, and booked jobs.

If you only remember one thing, remember this:

> Facebook is no longer one post type.
> Reels pull strangers in.
> Feed converts trust.
> Stories keep you remembered.

- bio that says:

## Content Priorities for Local Business

### What to post most often
1. **Job proof**
   - what failed
   - what was found
   - what was fixed
   - what the customer avoided by acting now

2. **Mechanic / operator POV**
   - what people ignore
   - common costly mistakes
   - field truths

3. **Local relevance**
   - city/service area references
   - weather conditions
   - route/corridor mentions
   - neighborhood/fleet-yard context

4. **FAQ education**
   - answer one question per post
   - short, direct, useful

5. **Customer proof**
   - review screenshot
   - job recap
   - testimonial clip

### What to post less often
- direct promotions
- generic memes
- stock graphics with no proof
- repeated link drops
- corporate-sounding announcements nobody cares about

## Weekly Posting Mix

Default baseline for local businesses:

- **3 Reels / week**
- **2 feed posts / week**
- **Stories on posting days**
- **0–2 organic link posts per month total** for non-Verified pages

If the team is small, minimum viable version:
- **2 Reels / week**
- **1 trust-building feed post / week**
- **3–5 Story frames on active days**

## Reel Rules

### What works now
- hook in first 1–3 seconds
- clear text overlay in safe zone
- real footage beats polished fake footage
- captions on screen
- audio or voiceover helps
- local/service context quickly visible
- same-day or near-real-time uploads usually beat stale recycled posts
- interest-fit and usefulness matter more than bait; the UTIS model punishes weak-match clickbait faster

### Reel quality rule
Optimize for:
- completion
- replays
- saves/shares
- strong audience match

Do **not** optimize for empty curiosity hooks that fail to deliver.
Facebook is surveying users more directly now, so low-satisfaction Reels get exposed faster.

### Hook types to test
- **Value promise**
  - "3 issues that cause expensive breakdowns for {{PRIMARY_AUDIENCE}} in {{CITY}}"
- **Statement of intent**
  - "Here’s what we check before a roadside no-start turns into a tow"
- **Question / invitation**
  - "Would you keep driving if your truck sounded like this?"

### Reel structure
1. Hook
2. Show the problem / proof
3. Explain 1–3 practical points
4. CTA

### Reel CTA examples
- "Need help now? Message the page."
- "Save this so you know what to check."
- "If your truck is doing this, call before it gets worse."

## Feed Post Rules

Feed exists to build trust and get comments.

Best feed formats:
- job recap with photo
- checklist post
- myth vs reality
- short story from the field
- testimonial / review proof
- local advisory update

### Feed post structure
1. First line = problem, lesson, or sharp observation
2. 1–3 short paragraphs or bullets
3. One clear ending CTA or question

### Good endings
- "Has this happened to you before?"
- "Want us to make a checklist for this issue?"
- "If your truck is showing these signs, message us."

## Story Rules

Stories are for recency and proximity.

Use Stories for:
- today’s jobs
- route/service-area updates
- weather / heat / breakdown conditions
- quick FAQs
- review screenshots
- polls / question stickers if manually posting native

Ideal run:
- 3 to 7 frames
- one job/proof frame
- one tip/FAQ frame
- one CTA frame

## Facebook Groups Strategy

For local brands, Groups can outperform Page-only posting for real discussion.

### Use groups carefully
- prioritize relevant local/community/trade groups
- adapt the post to the group
- do **not** spray the same promotional copy everywhere
- aim to be useful first

### Group post formats that work
- warning/awareness post
- practical checklist
- local incident lesson
- "here’s what we saw this week"
- answer to a common question

### Group rule
If the post reads like an ad, it loses.
If it reads like help from somebody who actually knows the work, it has a shot.

## Comment and Message Discipline

Facebook rewards responsiveness and local businesses win on trust.

### After posting
For the first 15–30 minutes:
- respond to every real comment
- keep replies warm and specific
- avoid dead-end replies like "thanks"
- ask one follow-up when natural

### Strong comment behavior
- answer the question
- add one useful detail
- invite next step if relevant

Example:
- weak: "Thanks"
- strong: "Appreciate it. If you’re hearing that under load, check it before it strands you — that’s when it gets expensive."

## Originality and AI Rules

Follow Meta’s 2026 originality and AI-media rules strictly.

### Avoid
- TikTok-watermarked reposts
- lazy cross-posts with no adaptation
- reaction content with no real value
- stolen clips with cosmetic edits only
- realistic AI-generated people/faces/scenes without disclosure
- AI-heavy content with no discernible human contribution if monetization matters

### Prefer
- original field footage
- original narration
- original analysis
- original job photos
- genuinely transformed repurposing
- real customer/context proof over synthetic visuals

### AI disclosure rule
If a post uses realistic AI-generated people-like media, disclose it.
For local service businesses, default to **real job photos, real crew footage, real locations** whenever possible.
That is safer for reach, safer for trust, and stronger for conversion.

If cross-posting from TikTok to Facebook:
- remove watermark
- rewrite caption
- adjust hook for Facebook
- add context/question for comments
- disclose realistic AI media where required

## Link Post Rules

Facebook links matter again, but they now have to be used **selectively**.

### Strategic reality in 2026
- referral traffic is stronger than it was for years
- but non-Meta Verified profiles should assume a practical cap of about **2 organic link posts per month**
- this means you should save link posts for your highest-intent moments

### Default rule
- for non-Verified local businesses, treat direct organic link posts as **scarce shots**
- prefer **0–2 per month**, not constant weekly linking
- if you need more action prompts, use:
  - Call Now
  - Message the Page
  - comment-to-DM workflows
  - Stories
  - pinned CTA posts

### Better pattern
- Reel or feed proof post first
- Story reinforcement
- then a strategic link/action post when intent is highest

### Best uses for scarce link posts
- emergency seasonal demand windows
- strong proof + clear service CTA
- lead magnets/checklists with obvious local value
- high-priority service pages

## Metrics That Matter

Track weekly:
- Primary outcome metrics:
  - calls
  - Page messages
  - booked jobs tied to Facebook activity
- Secondary performance metrics:
  - views
  - viewers
  - shares
  - saves if surfaced
  - comments
  - watch-through / completion patterns on Reels
  - top-performing hooks
  - top-performing proof formats
  - same-day vs delayed-post performance
- Vanity context metrics:
  - likes
  - reach

### Measurement rule for 2026
Do **not** use old 10-second video benchmarks.
Meta deprecated 10-second video metrics on Jan 26, 2026.
If you are comparing to old dashboards, reset your benchmark logic.

Do not optimize only for likes.
For local business, useful signal order is usually:
1. calls, messages, booked jobs
2. shares, saves, comments
3. views and viewers
4. watch behavior
5. likes and reach (context only)

## Operating Workflow

### Weekly workflow
1. Gather proof assets from the week
2. Sort into buckets: proof, FAQ, local, offer
3. Build next week’s 3 Reels + 2 feed posts
4. Decide whether this week uses a scarce link slot or a no-link CTA
5. Schedule where appropriate
6. Keep room for one live/reactive post

### Daily workflow
1. Check comments and messages
2. Reply fast
3. Note what hooks got comments or watch-through
4. Save new raw footage/proof into the content bank
